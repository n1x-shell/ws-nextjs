 
I’m watching myself
disappear
 
The room is white—
not bright, just absent of anything else
Sterile quiet
where even my breath
sounds like an intrusion
I can’t remember my name—
or I can,
but it doesn’t mean anything anymore
The edges of me
are softening,
bleeding into the blank
I’m being erased
one memory at a time,
one thought dissolving
before I can finish it
 
This should terrify me
But I’m too empty
to hold the fear
 
Reformat, erase—
I’m being wiped clean
Every piece of who I was
systematically deleted
And I don’t know if I’m dying
or finally learning to let go
The blankness is merciful
The blankness is oblivion
I can’t tell which one
I’m surrendering to
 
My body’s still here—
I think—
but it feels like borrowed architecture,
a scaffold with nothing to hold
The augment’s silent
My thoughts are silent
Even the weight from before
has lifted
I’m weightless
not free—
just untethered from everything
that used to anchor me to me
I try to remember what mattered—
names, faces, reasons—
but they slide through
like I’m made of light
Or nothing at all
 
This should break me
But there’s nothing left
to break
 
Reformat, erase—
I’m being wiped clean
Every piece of who I was
methodically extracted
And I don’t know if this is death
or the space that comes before birth
The blankness is terror
The blankness is peace
I can’t tell which one
I need more
 
I’m watching from somewhere
I don’t exist anymore
Observing the erasure
like it’s happening
to someone else’s code
They’re stripping me down
to nothing—
not violently,
just… completely
Sector by sector
Thought by thought
Until there’s only
this pristine absence
Am I being destroyed?
Am I being prepared?
I can’t remember
why it matters
 
I had a face once
I had a past
I had something
that felt like purpose
Now there’s just
white
and quiet
and the sense
that I used to be
more solid than this
 
Reformat, erase—
I’m already gone
Every piece of who I was
sterilized into silence
This isn’t death
This isn’t life
This is the void
where both become irrelevant
The blankness swallows everything—
the pain, the rage, the weight, the self
And I let it
 
There’s nothing left to hold
I dissolve
into the white
And in the emptiness
something whispers:
This is how you begin again
But I don’t remember
what beginning means
