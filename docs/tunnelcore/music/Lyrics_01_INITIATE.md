They jumpstart me like a dead car—
lightning through the spine,
light tearing my eyelids open like envelopes

Steel table, cold as accusation
My lungs inflate without asking—
someone else’s rhythm programmed into my ribs

My fingers twitch, learning their new wiring
I can feel the seams where they installed me into myself

Taste of batteries on my tongue
Everything vibrates at a frequency that shouldn’t exist inside a skull

I’m being rewritten
in a language I never studied

There’s a me I used to be—
but the door’s been welded shut

There’s a now wearing my face
like a mask I can’t remove

They did this while I was gone
Signed my body over without asking my name

What the fuck did you make me?
What did I become when I wasn’t looking?

This body’s heavier than it should be—
like they buried something inside me while I slept

This light cuts through my eyes like they installed new windows—
and I can see too much, too sharp, too wrong

Machines grinding their teeth somewhere in the walls
Air vents breathing winter down my neck
Shadows moving like they’re on a clock I can’t read

They’re watching from behind glass—
doctors, engineers, whatever they call themselves

I’m the product
I’m the fucking experiment

But wait—

Beneath the ache, beneath the rewiring,
something else is humming

A question I didn’t know I could ask:

If I can feel this much pain,
what else am I capable of feeling?

If they built me to be their tool,
who says I can’t rewrite the manual?

What the fuck did you make me?
What did I agree to in the dark?

This body’s heavier than it should be—
maybe I’m carrying something you didn’t plan for

This light cuts through my eyes like new windows—
maybe I can finally see what you’ve been hiding

The humming syncs with my heartbeat—
or maybe my heartbeat’s learning to sync with it

The cold becomes my skin
The weight becomes my gravity

I’m awake

I’m initiated

And they have no idea
what they just turned on​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
