# ZONE 10: INDUSTRIAL DRAINAGE

> Depth: Shallow undercity
> Location: Beneath the Industrial District
> Faction: Chrome Wolves (upper level) / None (lower level)
> Rooms: 10
> Level Range: 7–12 (Acts 2–3)
> Origin Point: None

-----

## OVERVIEW

Everything pours downhill. The Industrial District's factories run twenty hours a day producing Helixion's hardware — resonance amplifiers, mesh repeaters, neural interface components, the parts that build the Broadcast Tower. The manufacturing process generates waste: chemical runoff, coolant discharge, metal particulate slurry, and synthetic compounds that don't appear on any safety data sheet. This waste drains through grates in the factory floors into the drainage infrastructure beneath the district. The infrastructure was designed for water. It gets poison.

The Industrial Drainage is a two-layer zone. The upper drainage is Chrome Wolf territory — accessible, controlled, repurposed. The Wolves use the drainage tunnels the way they use the dead factories above: they claim what nobody else wants and make it work. Stash rooms carved from utility alcoves. A vault behind a locked gate. Supply corridors connecting Wolf operations that can't be linked on the surface. The upper drainage is the Chrome Wolves' basement — organized, guarded, functional. You can walk here if the Wolves let you. The chemicals are present but manageable — the runoff flows through channels, not across the floor.

The lower drainage is something else. Below the Wolf level, the tunnels descend into the original industrial infrastructure — pre-Helixion, pre-district, the foundations of whatever occupied this ground before the factories were built. The chemicals have been pooling here for decades. The air is corrosive. The water is toxic. The concrete is dissolving. What lives down here is adapted to the poison: corroded feral augments whose chrome is being eaten by the chemical exposure, their implants degraded, their pain constant. The lower drainage is where the city's waste goes to die, and it's taking the infrastructure with it.

The two layers connect through the Chemical Treatment Station — a pre-Helixion water purification facility that was designed to clean the drainage before it reached the water table. The station is broken. It's been broken for years. Nobody repaired it because nobody with the authority to repair it knows the lower drainage exists, and nobody who knows the lower drainage exists has the authority to repair it. The poison flows through the broken station, past the corroded tunnels, through the flooded gallery, and down into the Deep Drain — the lowest point in the shallow undercity, where everything converges before descending into the Abandoned Transit system below.

This is where the Parish's water comes from. The Drainage Nexus sits downstream. Every chemical the factories pour into the ground filters through these tunnels before reaching the Parish. The cough that the Parish members carry. The burning when they drink the filtered water. The slow poisoning of the only community that lives in the drainage system. It starts here.

## ATMOSPHERE

```
Sound:    Upper level — liquid flow through channels, deep and
          constant. Metal echoes from Wolf activity. Boots on
          grating. The distant thrum of factory machinery
          above, transmitted through the structure. Voices
          carry but the chemical air dampens them — words
          arrive muffled, stripped of consonants.

          Lower level — dripping. Slow, irregular dripping
          from surfaces that are dissolving. The hiss of
          chemical reactions — acid meeting metal, concrete
          fizzing where the runoff touches it. Something
          moving in the water. Something breathing in the
          dark. The corroded ferals make sounds that aren't
          speech and aren't animal. Something between.

Light:    Upper level — work lights strung by the Wolves.
          Orange and white. Adequate for navigation,
          deliberately dim in sections the Wolves want dark.

          Lower level — chemical luminescence. The runoff
          itself glows in places — yellow-orange, sickly,
          casting light that makes everything look infected.
          In the flooded gallery, the glow comes from below
          the water surface, illuminating from beneath.

Smell:    The defining sensory experience. Acrid, metallic,
          sharp enough to taste. Upper level is manageable
          — the ventilation still partially works. Lower
          level burns. Eyes water. Throat tightens.
          Prolonged exposure without protection causes
          nausea, disorientation, and HP drain. The smell
          is the zone's primary mechanic.
```

## ROOMS

### 1. INFLOW CHAMBER
**Where the poison enters. The factories' throat.**

```
> INDUSTRIAL DRAINAGE — INFLOW CHAMBER

The runoff pipe from the surface opens into a concrete
chamber — four meters high, eight meters across, the
ceiling a lattice of drainage pipes converging from every
factory above. The pipes discharge continuously. Yellow-
orange liquid pours from the largest pipe — the Assembly
Line's output, the worst of it. Smaller pipes contribute
lesser streams: coolant (blue-gray), metal slurry (black),
and something clear that smells like burning plastic.

The streams converge in a central channel that runs the
length of the chamber, flowing south. The channel is lined
with corrosion-resistant material that's losing the
battle — the lining is buckled, peeling, the concrete
beneath already eaten. Walkways on either side of the
channel provide passage. The walkways are wet with spray
and condensation. The air is sharp.

The Chrome Wolves have installed ventilation — a duct
system drawing air from the surface through a shaft. It
helps. The chemicals are present but breathable. Above
the chamber entrance, a spray-painted wolf skull marks
territory. Below the skull, smaller text: "BREATHE
SHALLOW."
```

- **Exits:** up (Industrial District — Runoff Channel, zone 3 r04), east (East Access), south (Wolf Checkpoint)
- **NPCs:**
  - `wolf_workers` — Chrome Wolves. 2. Loading and unloading supply crates from the surface. They use the inflow chamber as a logistics hub — goods come down through the drainage pipe access shaft, bypassing the streets. The workers are wearing chemical masks. They offer the player one if asked (reduces environmental damage on the lower level).
- **Enemies:** None (Wolf territory — safe if not hostile with the Wolves)
- **Objects:**
  - `factory_discharge` — Examine: "Watch the pipes. Each one drains a different factory. The largest — the Assembly Line's output — pours yellow-orange liquid in a continuous stream. TECH ≥ 6: The color indicates heavy metal contamination and synthetic polymer waste. The compounds are carcinogenic. The factories produce Broadcast Tower components. The Tower's construction generates waste that poisons the water supply for everyone who lives below the Industrial District. The production and the poisoning are the same process."
  - `corrosion_damage` — Examine: "The channel lining is failing. Designed for industrial water drainage — rain, condensation, sanitary waste. Not designed for the concentrated chemical cocktail Helixion's factories produce. The lining buckles and peels. The concrete beneath dissolves. TECH ≥ 5: At this rate of degradation, the channel wall will breach within two years. When it does, the runoff will saturate the surrounding soil. The water table contamination will be permanent."
  - `wolf_ventilation` — Examine: "A duct system running from the surface through a utility shaft. The Wolves installed it themselves — rough engineering, functional. It draws clean air from the surface and vents the chemical atmosphere. The system makes the upper drainage habitable. Without it, the concentration of airborne toxins would make extended presence dangerous. The Wolves care about their territory. The territory below the Wolves has no ventilation."
  - `breathe_shallow` — Examine: "Spray-painted beneath the Wolf skull. Practical advice. The chemical air is less concentrated above chest height — the heavier compounds settle. Breathing shallow, through the mouth, reduces exposure. The Wolves who work the upper drainage do this instinctively. They've adapted to the air the way the Parish has adapted to the water. The adaptation costs something. They don't talk about what."

### 3. WOLF CHECKPOINT
**Access control. The Wolves decide who goes deeper.**

```
> INDUSTRIAL DRAINAGE — WOLF CHECKPOINT

A reinforced gate across the main drainage corridor.
Chain-link topped with razor wire, a lockable door, and
two Chrome Wolves on the other side who aren't interested
in conversation until you've stated your business. The
checkpoint separates the inflow chamber — where anyone
might wander — from the stash corridor and vault beyond,
where only Wolf-approved traffic passes.

The gate is serious hardware. The Wolves who guard it
are serious people. But the checkpoint exists because
the Wolves need one point of control, and a single point
of control means a single point of failure. The drainage
tunnels branch. The pipes connect. The infrastructure
has redundancies the gate doesn't cover.
```

    - Location: Behind the gate. Clipboard in hand.
    - Faction: Chrome Wolves (logistics — manages the underground stash operation)

hardware that isn't Wolf-manufactured, isn't Helixion-

### 6. CHEMICAL TREATMENT STATION
**The broken filter. What should have protected everyone below.**

```
> INDUSTRIAL DRAINAGE — CHEMICAL TREATMENT STATION

The transition between levels. A pre-Helixion water
treatment facility — built when the Industrial District
was constructed, designed to purify drainage water before
it reached the water table. Four treatment tanks, each
one a cylinder five meters in diameter. Chemical
injection systems. Filtration banks. A control room
with manual gauges and physical switches.

The station is broken. Tank Two has a ruptured wall —
chemical runoff flows through it unfiltered, bypassing
the treatment process. Tank Four's filtration bank has
been clogged for years and nobody cleared it. The
chemical injection system is empty — the neutralizing
agents haven't been replenished since before Helixion
absorbed the district's infrastructure. The control
room lights still work because the station has its
own dedicated power circuit. Everything else is
failure.

The untreated runoff pours through the station and
continues downward — into the corroded tunnels, through
the flooded gallery, past the Parish outpost, and into
the Drainage Nexus. Everything the Parish drinks passed
through this room. This room could have cleaned it. This
room doesn't.

The station could be repaired. The infrastructure exists.
The tanks, the injection system, the filtration banks —
damaged but not destroyed. Someone with the right
knowledge and the right materials could make this work
again. Nobody has. Nobody who can repair it knows it
exists. Nobody who knows it exists can repair it.

Until now.
```

- **Exits:** up (Wolf Checkpoint area — ladder to upper level), south (Corroded Tunnels)
- **NPCs:**
  - **BRINE** — Parish Scout (QUESTGIVER / LORE / FACTION)
    - Location: In the treatment station's control room. Studying the equipment.
    - Faction: Parish (Drainage Nexus — sent upstream to investigate the water contamination source)
    - Disposition: Starts Neutral (0). Brine is cautious but purposeful — she's on a mission and allies are welcome.
    - Personality: Thirties. Lean, scarred. The Parish cough is audible when she speaks — a wet rattle in her chest that punctuates her sentences. She's been sent upstream by Elder Josiah (z08 r11) to find out why the water is getting worse. The Parish has noticed: the chemical concentration in their water supply has increased over the past year. More people are sick. The filtered water burns more than it used to. Something changed upstream.
    - What changed: the Assembly Line expanded production. More Broadcast Tower components, more waste, more poison flowing through the drainage. Brine has traced the contamination to the treatment station and understands that the broken station is the problem — or rather, the solution that nobody implemented.
    - She's not an engineer. She can identify the problem but not fix it. She needs someone who can. Or she needs parts, chemicals, expertise. She needs help, and the Parish doesn't have the resources to provide it.
    - Information: Brine knows the drainage system between here and the Nexus — the corroded tunnels, the flooded gallery, the routes the Parish uses to move between their territory and the upper drainage.     - Quests:
      - **"Purification"** (Tier 2-3): The treatment station can be repaired. It requires three things:

## DESIGN NOTES

**The Industrial Drainage is the game's accountability zone.** Everything the surface does has consequences that flow downhill. The factories produce. The chemicals drain. The treatment station breaks. The Parish drinks poison. The causal chain is physical — you can walk it, room by room, from the Inflow Chamber to the Parish Outpost. No zone in the game makes the relationship between surface power and underground suffering more literal.

**The Purification quest is the game's most impactful world change.** Repairing the Chemical Treatment Station permanently improves the Parish's water supply. NPCs acknowledge it. The cough eases. Cole's clinic sees fewer cases. Elder Josiah lives longer. The effect is visible and lasting. In a game full of moral ambiguity and institutional critique, this quest lets the player fix something. Actually fix it. The fix is complicated — it requires components from multiple sources and TECH skill — but the result is unambiguous. Clean water. Fewer sick people. The game needs this. Not every problem is structural. Some problems have wrenches.

**Strand's revelation is the game's most faction-disrupting lore bomb.** The Chrome Wolves aren't independent — they're Helixion contractors operating under the appearance of opposition. This doesn't make the individual Wolves' beliefs less real. Rade still believes in the pits. Torque still believes in the rooftops. Grath still believes in clean fighting. But Voss knows the arrangement. The rank and file don't. The player carrying this information to Iron Bloom triggers a faction crisis that forces every Wolf NPC to reckon with the gap between what they believe and what they serve. The revelation is devastating because it doesn't make the Wolves villains. It makes them victims of a different kind.

**The Substrate node in the flooded gallery is the zone's deepest lore touch.** A piece of the Substrate network, submerged in toxic chemicals, slowly dying. The 33hz signal intensifying — not broadcasting louder, but screaming. The chemicals from the factories above are killing a piece of the living infrastructure that predates the city. The Substrate is trying to heal. The damage is faster than the healing. Nobody who makes decisions about factory output knows this node exists. Nobody who knows it exists makes decisions about factory output. The gap between knowledge and power is the game's recurring tragedy.

**The Wolf vault's unidentified hardware — a natural Substrate resonance amplifier — raises the most dangerous question in the zone.** Where did Voss get a piece of the Substrate? The device is organic, grown, still active. It wasn't manufactured. It was harvested. Voss, who has an arrangement with Helixion, has a piece of living Substrate in his vault. Did Helixion give it to him? Did he take it? Is it part of the arrangement? The device hums at body temperature and resonates at 33hz. It might be tracking-capable — the Substrate might know where it is. The Wolves are keeping a piece of something ancient in a vault beneath the factories, and the something ancient might be aware of this.

**The manufacturing bypass is the zone's quietest horror.** The Assembly Line's dedicated drainage channel carries compounds that break down human neural tissue. The resonance amplifiers aren't just manufactured — they're grown from nervous system material. The workers on the Assembly Line aren't just calibrating machines. Their bodies are providing raw material through a process disguised as occupational exposure. Brenn's workers are being consumed. This data, given to Acre for analysis, provides evidence that connects to the Assembly Line questline in zone 3 and the Iron Bloom's case against Helixion in zone 12.
